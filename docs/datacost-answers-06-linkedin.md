**DataCost Answers #6: Is Your Cellphone Retention Offer a Good Deal—or Should You Switch?**

“We have a special offer if you stay” can sound attractive. But a lower monthly payment does not automatically mean a better deal.

Before accepting, make the retention offer and the best realistic alternative comparable:

- Use the same contract period.
- Compare equivalent phones—or price the phone separately.
- Separate anytime data from night, social or on-network allocations.
- Add upgrade, SIM, connection and administration fees.
- Add any settlement still owed to the old provider if you switch.
- Confirm when promotional discounts and bonus data end.
- Test alternative coverage where you actually use the phone.

Then calculate the full-term amount:

**Total cost = monthly price × number of payments + once-off costs + settlement**

A “free” phone, accessory or data bonus should only receive the value it has to you. If you would not have bought the extra, it should not decide the contract.

Most importantly, get the complete offer in writing. The start date, term, device, allocations, promotions, fees and exit rules should not exist only in a sales call.
