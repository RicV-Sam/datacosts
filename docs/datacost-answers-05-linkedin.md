**DataCost Answers #5: Your Cellphone Contract Ended — Should You Upgrade, Go SIM-Only, Choose Prepaid or Switch?**

An “upgrade available” message is not a deadline to renew.

When a cellphone contract reaches the end of its fixed term, separate four decisions:

- Does the phone genuinely need replacing?
- Is the current network still the best for coverage?
- Do you want postpaid billing, SIM-only or prepaid control?
- Are you willing to accept another 24- or 36-month commitment?

For an individual consumer, an expired fixed-term agreement generally continues month to month unless the customer directs termination or expressly agrees to another fixed term. That can create a useful comparison window—but check the new price because promotional data or discounts may fall away.

Before accepting an upgrade:

- Multiply the monthly price by the full term and add all once-off costs.
- Compare that total with keeping the current phone on SIM-only or prepaid.
- Ask for the contract end date, new agreement start date and any remaining device amount.
- Test another network’s coverage before switching.
- If keeping the number, start the port with the new provider before disconnecting the old service.
