**DataCost Answers #4: How Do You Cancel a Cellphone Contract in South Africa?**

Returning a phone, cancelling a service, stopping a debit order, and porting a number are four different actions. Confusing them can leave a customer paying for a line they thought was closed.

For an individual consumer, the practical starting point is a clear cancellation instruction in writing or another recorded form, with proof and a reference number. If the contract is still within its fixed term, ask for an itemised settlement quote first. If the term has expired, the service generally continues month to month until it is cancelled.

Before you cancel:

- Confirm the fixed-term end date, account number, mobile number, and device details.
- Request an itemised service and device settlement.
- State whether the number must move to prepaid, be ported, or be disconnected.
- Keep the cancellation notice, reference numbers, device-return receipt, and final bill.
- If the provider does not resolve the issue, complete its complaints process before escalating to ICASA or the National Consumer Commission, as appropriate.

The Consumer Protection Act allows a consumer to cancel a fixed-term agreement with 20 business days' written or other recorded notice, but a reasonable cancellation charge may apply. There is no universal rule that every charge is capped at 10%.
